Where Does Your Tax Money Actually Go?

In Fiscal Year 2024, the federal government spent $7 trillion. That's $7,000,000,000,000—a number so large it's almost meaningless. Let's make it real.

Where the Money Comes From

Notice something? The government collected $5.2 trillion but spent $7.0 trillion. That $1.78 trillion gap is the deficit—money borrowed that must be paid back with interest.

The Pizza Budget Game

Give kids 10 pepperonis (or coins) and ask them to "pay" for family needs. How many for food? Housing? Fun?

  • 3 pepperonis = Food (groceries)
  • 3 pepperonis = House (mortgage/rent)
  • 2 pepperonis = Car & activities
  • 1 pepperoni = Savings
  • 1 pepperoni = Treats & toys

Discussion: "What if we only had 8 pepperonis but still needed all these things?"

The Allowance Nation

Turn their allowance into a mini-government budget. If they get $20/week:

  • $10 goes to "taxes" (family savings)
  • $4 is "borrowed" (you loan them)
  • They decide how to spend $14
  • But they must pay back $4 + $1 interest next month

Discussion: "How does borrowing now affect what you can buy later?"

The Interest Rate Challenge

Use this page's data to explore real trade-offs:

  • Calculate: If the deficit adds 1% to mortgage rates, how much more does a $400,000 house cost over 30 years?
  • Research: What were mortgage rates when your parents bought their first home?
  • Debate: Should the government spend less or tax more to reduce debt?

Answer: 1% higher rate = ~$86,000 more in interest over 30 years!

South Florida Home Hunt

Make real estate tangible with a family activity:

  • Browse Zillow together for homes in your area
  • Use a mortgage calculator to see monthly payments at different rates
  • Compare: What could you afford at 3% vs 7%?
  • Discuss: Why do almost 50% of Palm Beach buyers pay cash?

Discovery: Higher rates = smaller homes OR higher payments

Cash Is King

When nearly half of buyers pay cash, mortgage rates matter less. Wealthy migrants from NY and CA sell high-priced homes up north and buy here without financing.

The Great Migration

IRS data shows FL gains more high earners than any state. NY, CA, and NJ account for 47% of out-of-state buyers in Miami—bringing serious purchasing power.

Global Safe Haven

South Florida's foreign buyer share is 5x the national average. For international investors, Miami real estate represents a stable, dollar-denominated asset.
